As is shown above, there are two men with different attitudes to books. The man in the left picture leans on the soaf lazily in front of book shelves full of books, saying "I have so many books". By comparison, the man in the right picture is reading a book and making a feasible plan to read twenty books this year. Below the pictures, there is a caption which reads "having books and reading books".
In such a society rich in resources, books are no more scarce. Some people identify possessing konwledge with having a host of books, who seldom settle down to read books. In my opinion, we should learn from the right man to make specific plans and put it into practice. As an old saying goes, "Konwledge comes from practice". It tells us that the practical action is indispensable to the acquisition of knowledge. It is true that we need "having books" before we read them, however, we will never derive the wisdom from them untill we begin to read.
In conclution, we should avoid to confining us to the confort zone where we merely "possessing books". Instead, it is advisible for us to deal everything in a down-to-earth way. Just as Present Xi once put it, "Empty talk would lead a country astray, and hard work can revitalize(振兴) a nation", only through continuing efforts, can individuals advance steadily and the Chinese nation achieve great rejuvenation.
