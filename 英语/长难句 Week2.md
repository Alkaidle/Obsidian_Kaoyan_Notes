**Day 15**

While even the ==modestly educated== sought an elevated tone when they put pen to paper before the 1960s, even the most ==well regarded== writing since then has sought to capture spoken English on the page.


虽然在1960年代之前连文化水平最低的人在写字前都要寻求一种情绪高昂的语调，但从那之后甚至就连最相关的写作也能在文字中找到口语的痕迹

在19世纪60年代以前，尽管受过一般教育的人在动笔写作前都要寻求一种文雅的语调，但从那之后就连最受推崇的作品也会追求在文章中口语化表达

在1960年代以前，即使是教育程度不高的人写作时也倾向于使用较为庄重的文风，而自那以后，即便是最受赞誉的写作也往往追求展现英语口语的自然风貌。

**Day 16**

As a linguist, he acknowledges that all varieties of human language, including non-standard ones like Black English, can be powerfully expressive—there exists ==no== language or dialect in the world that ==cannot== convey complex ideas.

作为一个语言学家，他认为人类语言的所有变体，包括像非洲裔美国人英语那样的非标准的形式，都具有很强的表达能力—这个世界上没有哪种语言或方言不能够表达复杂的思想


**Day 17**

Russians have a deep love for their own language and ==carry== large chunks of memorized poetry in their heads, while Italian politicians ==tend to== ==elaborate speech== that would seem old-fashioned to most English-speakers.

俄罗斯人深深热着本国的语言，许多人脑中记诵着大量的诗歌，而意大利政客们则往往以华丽的辞藻发表演讲，这种风格在大多数英语使用者看来颇为老派

**Day 18**

Mr. McWhorter acknowledges that formal language is not strictly necessary, and proposes ==no radical education reforms==—==he is really grieving over the loss of something beautiful more than useful==.

McWhorter 认为正式的语言并非严格必需，并且没有提出任何激进的教育改革措施—比起实用性，他更为失去了美感而感到惋惜

**Day 19**

Rodriguez notes that children in remote villages around the world are fans of superstars like Arnold Schwarzenegger and Garth Brooks, yet “some Americans fear that immigrants living within the United States remain somehow immune to the nation’s assimilative power.”

Rodriguez 指出，在世界各地偏远村庄的孩子们都是Arnold Schwarzenegger 和 Garth Brooks 等超级巨星的粉丝，然而，“一些美国人担心，居住在美国的移民似乎仍不受这个国家同化能力的影响”

**Day 20**

Today, surrounded by promises of easy happiness, we need art to tell us, as religion once did, Memento mori: remember that you will die, that everything ends, and that happiness comes not in denying this but in living with it.

如今，人们生活中充斥着对轻易获得幸福的许诺，但如同曾经宗教的作用一样，我们需要艺术来提醒我们——Memento mori，记住你终将死去，一切终会结束，幸福并非来自否定这个事实，而是与之共处

**Day 21**

After six months of arguing and final 16 hours of hot parliamentary debates, Australia’s Northern Territory became the first legal authority in the world to allow doctors to take the lives of incurably ill patients who wish to die.

经过6个月的争论以及最后16个小时国会激烈的辩论后，澳大利亚北领地成为世界上首个得到合法的、允许医生结束那些身患绝症并希望死亡的病人生命的地区
